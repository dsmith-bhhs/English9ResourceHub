EDIT NOTE 3: RESTRICTIVE & NONRESTRICTIVE CLAUSES
A dependent clause that presents added information not needed to keep the
meaning of the sentence intact (a nonrestrictive clause) is set off by commas.
EX. The farm, which has a beautiful yellow barn, sells apples and pumpkins.
A dependent clause that comes in the middle or the end of a sentence and is
necessary to the meaning (a restrictive clause) is not set off by commas.
EX. The farm that we passed a mile back sells apples and pumpkins.
Please Note: Many people incorrectly use that and which interchangeably. Use
the word that to introduce restrictive clauses and the word which to introduce
nonrestrictive clauses.

EDIT NOTE 4: APPOSITIVES
An appositive is a word or group of words that follows a noun and identifies it
or gives more information. An appositive is set off by commas.
EX:
Mrs. Aber and Mrs. Salvato, our English teachers, are friends.
We visited the Adams Library, one of the oldest in America.
Rule: When an appositive is so closely related to the word it modifies that it
appears to be part of that word, no comma is necessary. An appositive of this
kind is called a restrictive appositive. It is usually one word. (Hint: when the
appositive is before the noun, no comma is needed).
EX:
My nephew Jim
The American gymnast Simone Biles
My friend Julia

EDIT NOTE 5: SEMICOLONS
Vary sentences by joining two closely related sentences with a semicolon and
no conjunction.
EX:
She stared intently at the stranger; all eyes followed her as she moved toward him.
The movie was filmed in France; the producers rented all the homes in a small village for
two months.
Use a semicolon and a comma in joining two closely related sentences that
include a conjunctive adverb, such as “however," "otherwise," "therefore," or
a phrase like “for example,” “in fact," or "for instance.”`
EX:
At first, the painting was considered worthless; however, it recently sold for one million
dollars.
Universities have many challenges; for example, bringing together diverse students.

EDIT NOTE 7: POSSESSIVES
A possessive form of a singular noun is made by adding an apostrophe and s.
('s). EX. doctor's, family's, James’s (Note: James’ is also acceptable)
If a plural noun does not end in “s”, add an apostrophe and “s” to form the
possessive ('s). EX: children's, men's, people's
If a plural noun does end in “S”, add only the apostrophe to form the
possessive.
EX: guitarists’, parents’, senators'
When the names of two or more persons are used to show joint ownership,
only the name of the last person mentioned is given the possessive form. Add
an apostrophe or an apostrophe and “S” in accord with the spelling of that
name.
EX: Mom and Dad's idea, Michael and Anna's project
If the names of two or more persons are used to show separate ownership,
each name is given the possessive form.
EX: Spielberg's and Lucas's films, Marie's and Jonathan's essays

EDIT NOTE 9 -SUBJECT -VERB AGREEMENT
Subject-verb agreement means that the subject and the verb must agree in
number.
You must be able to recognize whether a pronoun is singular or plural to
determine which verb form to use.
Singular Pronouns: each, either, neither, one, everyone, everybody, no one, nobody,
anyone, anybody, someone, somebody
Plural Pronouns: several, few, both, many
The pronouns some, all, most, any, and none may be either singular or plural.
EX.
Some of the food was eaten. (quantity)
All of the furniture looks comfortable. (quantity)
Some of the apples were eaten. (number)
All of the chairs look comfortable. (number)
These pronouns are singular when they refer to a quantity of something and
plural when they refer to a number of things thought of individually.

EDIT NOTE 10: COMPOUND SUBJECTS
When a compound subject is joined by “and,” the verb is plural.
EX. Mood, character, and plot are elements of the short story.
When a compound subject is joined by “or” or “nor,” the verb agrees with the
subject nearer the verb.
EX. Neither the speaker nor the listeners were intimidated by the protesters.
EX. Neither the listeners nor the speaker was intimidated by the protesters.
