Philosophy Statement: The Byram Hills mission endeavors to create productive and responsible citizens and leaders of the
twenty-first century; in helping the district achieve its mission, the English department has integrated modern technology into the
framework of its work with students. With the recent proliferation of artificial intelligence comes challenges and complications for
the teaching of reading and writing. If students are to be successful graduates of Byram Hills High School, they will need to
understand the benefits and limitations of artificial intelligence. Ultimately, the department aims to help students understand how to
use AI tools responsibly while also providing students with the requisite skills to be independent thinkers, readers, and writers.

Sample Prompts using PREP (Prompt, Role, Explicit, Parameters):
Students are Permitted to Use the Following Prompts:
Research and Writing
● Retrieving Basic Information
○ I am interested in researching _______________, and I need to gather some basic information to help me understand the topic
better. Act as a research assistant. Provide a brief overview of what _______________ is, including its causes, effects, and some
current statistics. Make sure the information is clear and concise, suitable for a high school student. Limit the overview to 300
words and provide citations for any statistics mentioned.
● Brainstorming Topics
○ I am interested in writing an essay on _______________, but I'm not sure what specific topic to choose. Take on the role of an
academic advisor. Provide a list of five possible topics that are relevant, along with a brief description of each. The descriptions
should highlight why each issue is significant and could make for a compelling essay topic. Keep each description under 100
words.
● Locating Resources and/or Citation Information
○ I need to find credible resources for my paper on ____________________. Act as a research librarian. Identify three academic articles
or studies that provide different perspectives on this topic. Include the full citation for each resource in MLA format. Ensure the
resources are published within the last five years and are available through public databases or online journals.
● Editing for Spelling, Grammar, and Usage Errors
○ I have written an essay on ____________________, and I need help with proofreading. Serve as a professional editor. Review the
essay for any spelling, grammar, or usage errors. Provide corrected sentences where necessary and explain why the changes were
made. Focus only on mechanical errors, not on content or argumentation. Make sure the feedback is clear and instructive.
● Creating Writing Prompts
○ I want to develop some writing prompts for my classmates to help them explore ____________________. Assume the role of an
English teacher. Create three detailed writing prompts that encourage deep analysis of ____________________. Each prompt should
include a specific area of focus and should encourage at least 300 words of response.

Sample Prompts using PREP (Prompt, Role, Explicit, Parameters):
● Sample Opposite-Argument Brainstorm
○ Good morning. I'm a ninth-grade student about to embark on a research project called the opposite-argument paper. As part of the
project, I will be asked to explore an issue that has opposing viewpoints, identifying credible sources to help me understand the
related arguments. I'm really interested in the topic of __________________. Can you give me a list of possible topics to explore?
After each topic, include a question that will help me understand the opposing viewpoints; for example, you could say something
like "Should the United States grant asylum to victims of political persecution?" Limit your responses to 10 different topics, and
provide me with links for my initial research. Be sure to include links that will give me information on both sides of the argument.
Include more than one link per topic, if necessary. Please restrict the links to newspaper articles that have been published within
the last calendar year. Thank you.
Reading
● Defining Complex Vocabulary, Difficult Concepts, or Translating Text
○ I am struggling to understand the concept of ____________________ as it relates to literature. Take on the role of a tutor. Explain the
concept of ____________________ in simple terms and provide an example of how it is used in a literary context, such as in the
works of [author’s name]. Keep the explanation under 200 words and use accessible language that a high school student would
understand.
● Suggesting Options for Independent Reading (IR)
○ I’m looking for a book to read independently that will challenge me and align with my interest in ____________________. Serve as a
librarian or book club advisor. Suggest three books that fit within the ____________________ genre, with a brief description of each.
Explain why each book would be a good fit for an independent reading project. Make sure the suggestions are suitable for a high
school reader and are books that have been published in the last 10 years.
Presentations
● Providing Audio-Visual Aids
○ I need to create a presentation on ____________________ and would like to include some visual aids. Act as a multimedia consultant.
Recommend three types of visual aids (e.g., infographics, maps, timelines) that would effectively convey ____________________
Include a brief explanation of how each visual aid would enhance the presentation. Ensure the recommendations are suitable for a
10-minute presentation to a high school audience.

Sample Prompts using PREP (Prompt, Role, Explicit, Parameters):
Test Preparation
● Creating Study Guides
○ I’m preparing for a test on ____________________ and need a study guide to help me review. Take on the role of a study skills coach.
Create a detailed study guide that includes [key themes, character analysis, important quotes, and a summary of each act]. The
guide should be structured in a way that is easy to follow. Limit the study guide to 1,000 words, ensuring it covers the most critical
elements needed for a high school test.
The Following Prompts Require Teacher Permission:
Research & Writing
● Providing Substantive Feedback That Suggests Improvements to Arguments and/or Analysis
○ I’ve written a draft of an argumentative essay on ____________________, but I’m not sure if my arguments are strong enough. Act as
a writing coach. Provide detailed feedback on the strength of my arguments, suggesting specific areas where I can improve my
reasoning or provide additional evidence. Highlight any logical fallacies or weak points in the analysis. Focus on the content and
logic of the essay, and avoid making any changes to the writing style or grammar.
● Generating Sentence-Starters
○ I’m having trouble starting my essay on the symbolism in _____________________. Serve as a writing mentor. Provide three different
sentence starters that could help me begin my essay. Each sentence starter should introduce a different aspect of the symbolism
in the novel. Keep each sentence starter concise but impactful, and ensure they are varied enough to give me different options for
how to begin.
● Evaluating the Credibility or Bias of Sources
○ I found several articles on ____________________, but I’m not sure which ones are credible. Take on the role of a media literacy
expert. Evaluate the credibility and potential bias of three sources I provide. Offer a brief explanation of the reliability of each
source, considering factors such as the author’s qualifications, the publication, and any evidence of bias. Keep each evaluation
under 100 words, and focus on helping me determine which sources are trustworthy for an academic paper.

Sample Prompts using PREP (Prompt, Role, Explicit, Parameters):
● Providing a General Outline
○ I’m struggling to organize my ideas for an essay on ____________________. Act as an essay planner. Create a general outline for the
essay, including an introduction, three main points with subpoints, and a conclusion. The outline should help me structure my
thoughts and ensure my essay is coherent. Make sure the outline is flexible enough for me to adapt, but detailed enough to give
clear direction.
Reading
● Summarizing Classroom Texts
○ I need a summary of the main events in ____________________. Take on the role of a literature tutor. Provide a concise summary of
the key events that take place in ____________________. Include important quotes and explain their significance. Keep the summary
under 200 words and focus on the most critical moments that are likely to be discussed in class.
● Identifying Significant Quotations
○ I’m writing an essay on the theme of _______________ in ____________________ and need help identifying key quotations. Serve as a
literary analyst. Identify three significant quotations from ____________________ that relate to the theme of _______________. For each
quote, explain its context in the story and its relevance to the theme. Each explanation should be around 50 words, providing
enough detail to understand why the quote is important.
● Leveling Reading Material
○ I’m interested in reading more about ____________________, but I found texts that are not at a high school reading level. Act as a
literacy expert. Revise the texts I’ve found so that they are appropriate for a high school reader. Provide a brief description of each
text. Ensure the revised texts are both informative and engaging, suitable for someone in the 9th to 12th grade. Ask me for the link
to the first text and wait for my response. Then provide me an appropriately summarized version of the text. After each summary,
ask me for another text and repeat the process.
